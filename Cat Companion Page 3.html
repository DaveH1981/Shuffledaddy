A Cat Companion Primer

Some things to consider before adopting a cat:

Make Sure Everyone Is On Board

If you live with your family, talk to them before adopting a cat. Make sure they understand the responsibility in caring for an animal and are willing to help out. In some cases, it may be helpful to make a chart that states who is responsible for what part of the cat's care. This way, everything is clear and there is no confusion. If you have roommates, be sure to check with them before getting a cat. If you rent, ask your landlord if you are unsure about whether cats are allowed.

Be Prepared

It is important to have the supplies you will need before the cat arrives. Some basic items include:

  1. A litter box with litter and cat pan liners

  2. Appropriate cat food plus food and water bowls

  3. Safe toys to keep your cat entertained

  4. A soft bed lined with a blanket or towel

  5. Grooming tools such as a good brush and nail clippers

      6. Give Your Cat Her Space

      Find a low-traffic room or area of your home and designate that as your cat's space. Arrange her food and water bowls, litter box, and bed in an inviting manner. Scatter some cat toys around and clear out nearby areas such as windowsills. This will help your cat feel welcome as she adjusts to her new home.

      Take It Slow

      When you first bring your new cat home, give her some alone time before you play with her. If you have other animals living in your home, wait a few days before introducing them to your cat. Even then, supervision is important when your cat is interacting with your other pets. Also, hold off on having other people meet your cat until she is eating and going to the bathroom regularly. To aid in this, avoid changing your cat's food and litter brands for the first few weeks or so. Even after this point, any change should be a slow transition from one kind of food or litter to another.

      Cat-Proof Your Home

      Last but not least, please ensure that your home is reasonably free from hazards that could endanger your cat. Some examples of hazards are toxic houseplants, loose items that could be chewed or swallowed, and loose electrical wires. It is also helpful to keep items such as toilet paper, paper towels, and facial tissues out of the reach of your cat. This will help avoid a messy situation.
